Our Mission

Manufacturing for Independence: The Ham Group’s New Era
As of January 2026, the Ham Group has redefined what it means to be an industrial conglomerate in East Africa. Moving beyond its roots in real estate, the group now positions itself as a "total ecosystem provider." Its manufacturing arm doesn't just build factories; it builds independence. By integrating Ham Agro-Processing with its own financial vehicle, Ham Agro Bank, the group has created a model where manufacturing is fueled by local produce, processed with local labor, and exported as premium global brands. This "2026 Vision" is centered on the belief that true sovereignty for any nation begins on the factory floor.

Our Values

In 2026, the Ham Group has moved beyond being a collection of businesses to becoming a purpose-driven institution. By anchoring its manufacturing operations in these five values, the group has successfully transitioned from a regional player to a global industrial benchmark. The focus on Value Addition and Self-Reliance has allowed the group to insulate itself from global supply chain shocks, while its commitment to Sustainability and Integrity has opened doors to high-value European and American markets that demand ethical manufacturing. In this new era, the Ham Group's values serve as a blueprint for how indigenous African companies can dominate the industrial landscape through 2026 and beyond.

Our Vision

The Horizon of 2026: Ham Group’s Vision for a New Africa"
As we navigate 2026, the Ham Group of Companies has moved its goalposts from regional success to global influence. The group’s vision is no longer just about building buildings or factories; it is about re-engineering the African economy. Under the leadership of Dr. Hamis Kiggundu, the vision has crystallized into a pursuit of "Industrial Sovereignty." In 2026, this means creating a self-sustaining ecosystem where the Ham Agro-Processing Industrial Plant acts as the heart of a massive network of farmers, engineers, and tech experts. The vision is clear: by the end of the decade, the Ham Group intends to have closed the trade deficit gap by ensuring that the continent exports more finished goods than it imports. This is more than a business strategy; it is a 2026 manifesto for a continent that is finally taking its seat at the head of the global industrial table.
